Laundry Connect

The Problem

How do I make the process of doing laundry in a college dorm less frustrating and more efficient for students?

The Tools

Ideating: Miro

Prototyping: Paper and Pen, and Adobe XD

Testing: X-box Game Bar, and Python

Empathize

The Process

In order to get a feel for how students were currently doing their laundry in the dorms and what problems they were facing, I interviewed five college students who were actively living and doing laundry in the dorms. This was to give me a broader understanding of the problem at hand and to uncover what could be improved via the introduction of an app. I found through this process that a lot of students were unhappy with the communication in the dorms. For instance, students felt like they had no way to contact people who were leaving their laundry for long periods of time and thus they would have to move the laundry themselves. I kept the answers from the students close at mind when coming up with solutions.

Questions

Ideate

Brainstorming

Using my interviews I create a mind-map of potential solutions in Miro. I pulled a list of functions I felt the app had to complete based off what students were lacking in the dorms and then I branched off creating specific solutions to the problems and then breaking those solutions down further. I then used this mind map as a template when creating my prototypes.

Prototype

Low Fedility

Before creating my high fedility prototype I did some rough sketches to quickly compare some different solutions that I had come up with. I made sure to keep the sketches quick as to not spend too much time on one solution.

High Fedility

I used Adobe XD when creating my high fidelity prototype. I took the quick sketches from my low fidelity and decided which would be the best for my actual solution and then made them in Adobe XD. Here I was able to introduce more functionality to my app and create a working prototype.

Test

Process

In order to test my app I recruited five more college students who were currently living in the dorms. The hypotheses I was trying to test were: Is my app intuitive? Do the users understand how to use the app to use the laundry machines? Does the app help walk the user through the different features? And do people know how to navigate to the help page? I then had my users do a list of tasks that would test each of my hypotheses.

Tasks

  1. Pay to use Washer 2.
  2. Reserve a washer and dryer combo for October 20th from 2-4pm.
  3. Report Washer 1 as broken
  4. Report a found item
  5. Claim the shirt in lost and found.
  6. Create a new chat.
  7. Navigate to the help page.

Findings

I found that my app was intuitive like I thought but there were areas where the user got tripped up consistently. I found my users had a hard time finding the menu initially because of how my homepage was set up. This is where users paused the most. However the help page was easy to navigate to, many of my users confirmed that the question mark was the right choice to make for my app.
